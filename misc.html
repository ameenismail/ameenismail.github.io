trivia trainer

I really like the game show Jeopardy!, and I am a big fan of Trivia Trainer, which allows you to go through old Jeopardy! questions by category. The website has no built-in support for keyboard shortcuts, so I wrote a userscript for this purpose. Here's the code on my GitHub.

As I've written it you can show or hide the answer with the up arrow or the T key, and use the other arrow keys to click the "correct", "incorrect", and "don't know" buttons. But it's easy to customize it to use whatever keys you like.

Also, if you're unfamiliar with userscripts, they are bits of code that modify a webpage. You'll need to install a browser extension such as Greasemonkey or Tampermonkey; then create a new userscript, copy and paste the code I linked above, and save it.

recipes

Here are a couple of recipes I like.

chocolate cookies

Wet ingredients:

  • 1 egg
  • 1/3 cup canola oil
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 tsp. vanilla extract

Dry ingredients:

  • 1/3–1/2 cup cocoa powder
  • 2/3 cup flour
  • 1/4 tsp. baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp. salt

Bake at 350 Fahrenheit for around 10–12 minutes. If you put a lot of cocoa powder in you might need to add a tablespoon or two of oil or water to form a proper cookie dough.

brownies

Ingredients:
  • 1/2–3/4 cup canola oil
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 3/4 cup cocoa powder
  • 3 eggs
  • 3/4 cup flour

Heat the oil in the microwave for around 20 seconds, until it's warm. Then mix in the sugar and cocoa, and microwave it again for another 15–20 seconds. (This heating really makes a difference! I think it's because it helps the sugar dissolve better.) Mix very well. Add the eggs and mix them in, then gently fold in the flour.

You can optionally add vanilla extract and salt, as well as mix-ins like chocolate chips or nuts. Some people say chocolate tastes flat and boring without any salt. I think those people just don't like chocolate enough.

Anyway, scrape the batter into a pan (I use a 9 inch by 13 inch glass pan, but a smaller pan would probably be fine too). Bake at 350 Fahrenheit for about 20 minutes, maybe a little less if you like your brownies underdone in the centre.
